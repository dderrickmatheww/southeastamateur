About the league

The Southeast Amateur League is a competitive video game league that provides an authentic tournament expereince. S.A.L. aims to become a stepping stone for kids, teenagers, and adults to becoming professional eSport players. Our goal is to provide a platform to anyone that would like to compete in an 'eSport-like' environment. Have you ever wondered how it would feel to win a tournament playing your favorite games? What about for prizes? What about with your friends? Create your team, compete againsts other teams, and see if you can take home cash, prizes, or even the S.A.L. (Southeast Amateur League) trophy.


The league is still under developement but we will still be hosting stand alone tournaments for teams to compete for cash and prizes. This will eventually turn into the S. A. L.

If you are interested in competeing in these tournaments click the compete tab above and fill out the information.

FAQ

Why would I want my kid playing video games?

There are many studies on this topic and one that we take very seriously here at S.A.L. but it simply isn't true. One of the more recent studies by the University of Oxford shows that there is no correlation between violence video games and adolescents. Click here for more on the study. This is one of many different studies by a variety of different cohorts that prove this.

Infact its almost the oppisite. Adolescents that play games tend to actually be more intelligent according to York University. "They said they simply establish a correlation between skill at certain online games of strategy and intelligence." more on this study here.

S.A.L.'s goal is to provide a healthy environment for individuals to grow their sportsman-ship and social-skills. This expereince is not just about the video games, it's about the environment/platform that S.A.L. provides.

Are there rules?

Yes there are strict rules on equipment, conduct, and gameplay. If a player is caught breaking one of these rules, said player will be disqualified from the tournament. Depending on the circumstance may be asked to leave. Permanant bans are not an option as of right now but could be possible in the future.

Does it cost?

Yes but it will depend on the tournament. There will be an entry fee and a player fee. Entry fee is to come in and watch/hang out/support fellow players. Player fee is to actually compete in the tournament itself. Part of the player fee goes towards the championship pot so that whoever wins splits the championship pot amoungst their team, along with other prizes. As of right now, S.A.L. is only doing single game tournaments but will eventually create a league where we do seasons. Once we get to seasons the pricing will change (for the better) but we will cross that bridge once we get there.
